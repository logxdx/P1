Abstract

This document describes the design and fabrication of a low-cost, versatile function generator (FG) using the Espressif Systems 32 mi- crocontroller. The FG generates sine, square, and triangle waves up to 1 MHz, with basic AM and FM modulation. The design focuses on affordability and practicality, offering a valuable tool for students, hobbyists, and small-scale developers. Using readily available compo- nents, the project aims to provide a fully functional and cost-effective Function Generator (FG) that meets the requirements of first-year electrical students in academic settings.

  1. Project Statement

Design a cost-effective FG within a INR 1000 budget, replicating the technical specifications and front panel layout of the ELP101 FG.

  1. Motivation

The primary need addressed by this project is the development of a fully functional and cost-effective FG that meets the requirements of first-year electronics students in academic settings. Commercially available function generators are essential tools in electronics labs. However, they are often extremely expensive. This high cost limits their accessibility, especially for resource-constrained institutions.

This project focuses on providing a user-friendly device that incorporates all the essential features required for foundational learning while maintaining a cost cap of INR 1000. The design closely resembles the layout of the ELP101 FG, significantly lowering the financial barrier to accessing high-quality educational tools and promoting inclusivity and hands-on learning.

  1. Future Work

The function generator (FG) being developed provides fundamental signal generation capabilities suitable for introductory experiments, but it does not include advanced functionalities such as arbitrary waveform generation, higher modulation capabilities, or advanced signal processing options that are often found in pre- mium models. Additionally, the operational frequency range is capped at 1 MHz, which limits its application to experiments requiring higher frequencies or greater precision.

These limitations show opportunities for future improvements. Future versions can incorporate advanced features and capabilities.This will help meet complex academic and research needs while ensuring the design evolves to serve a wider range of uses.

  1. Requirements

  1. Inputs Requirements

  • Microcontroller: The Espressif Systems 32 provides powerful dual-core processing, built-in Wi- Fi/Bluetooth, and efficient signal control for the operation of versatile function generators.
  • Input controls : User-friendly controls for adjusting frequency, amplitude, and waveform selection. Use a rotary Rotatory Encoders connected to the microcontroller for precise input.
  • Frequency Range
  • Sine Wave : 0.1Hz to 3MHz
  • Square Wave and Triangle Wave: 1kHz to 1MHz
  • Web-based user interface for remote control and monitoring (non-functional).

  1. Outputs Requirements

  • Output waveforms
  • Functional: Sine, square and triangle waves.
  • Non-Functional: Pulse and ramp waves.
  • Adjustable output amplitude from 2 V to 20 V (Peak-to-Peak Voltage).
  • Constant output impedance, ensuring a reliable signal regardless of the impedance of the external circuit (50Ω).
  • Modulation features
  • Non-Functional: modulation capabilities Amplitude Modulation and Frequency Modulation (FM).
  • Attenuation feature for precise reduction in signal amplitude (non-functional).

  1. Power Requirements

  • The device should be powered using a standard AC power source (220V/50Hz).
  • Low power consumption (20W) to enable portability and extended operation.

  1. Logistic Requirements

  • The total cost of the components must not exceed Rs. 1000.
  • Components should be readily available in the market or through common suppliers.

  1. Environmental Requirements

  • The device should function effectively within standard laboratory conditions (temperature: 25–30°C, humidity: 30–50%, pressure: 101.3 kPa).
  • Durable design to withstand regular usage in a laboratory environment.

  1. Site (Usage Site) Requirements

  • Suitable for use in laboratory settings, specifically for ELP101 experiments.
  • Lightweight (1-3 kg) and compact (240 x 186 x 76 mm) with a convenient handle for easy relocation within laboratory spaces.

  1. Structural Requirements

  • The front panel layout should mimic the Function Generator used in ELP101.
  • Small display for user interaction:
  • Functional: A 16×2 Liquid Crystal Display (LCD).
  • Non-Functional: Web interface for parameter monitoring.
  • Compact and lightweight design for portability.

  1. Time Requirements

  1. Design Time Requirements

  • The prototype design and implementation must be completed within six weeks, as specified.

  1. Time to Market Requirements

  • Rapid assembly and testing should allow for use in academic settings with minimal delay.

  1. Lifetime Requirements

  • The device should have a durable life cycle, capable of functioning for at least two years under normal laboratory use.

  1. End of Life Requirements

  • Device components should be easy to recycle or dispose of in an environmentally responsible manner.

  1. How was the Assignment done

The assignment of tasks to each subgroup followed a structured and collaborative approach, aligned with our verticals: Research and Development, Enclosure Design and Fabrication, Firmware, and Docu- mentation. Tasks were assigned based on skill mapping, ensuring members with relevant expertise (e.g., CAD for enclosure design, electronics for circuit simulation, and technical writing for documentation) were allocated to their respective areas. Each vertical was broken down into specific tasks, such as prototyping and testing in R&D, 3D modeling and material procurement for enclosure fabrication, circuit design and simulation, and comprehensive documentation of processes and results. Collaborative team meetings en- sured clear responsibilities, balanced workloads, and minimized overlaps. A reserve team was identified for contingency planning, and regular reviews allowed for progress tracking and adjustments. This methodology leveraged individual strengths, fostered teamwork, and ensured efficient project execution across all verticals.

  1. Surplus Manpower and Contingency Plan

To ensure the smooth progression of the project, a reserve team of cross-trained members was identified. This team was equipped with basic skills in CAD modeling, electronics troubleshooting, and documentation, allowing them to intervene if any of the primary team members were unavailable. Key deployment scenarios included:

  • Delays: Redirecting reserves to critical tasks like PCB soldering or enclosure assembly.
  • Design flaws: Assist in rapid prototyping and design adjustments.
  • Software Issues: Debugging code or firmware using open source tools like PlatformIO.

  • Throw Test: The enclosure should withstand being thrown and caught during a game of catch.

  1. Challenges and Solutions
  • Material Choice: After deliberation between ABS plastic and acrylic, acrylic was chosen due to its ease of laser cutting and cost savings over 3D printing with ABS plastic.
  • Component Attachment: Decided to use threading, screws, and silicone adhesive for attaching components. While this makes the machine less repairable and accessible from the inside without a heat gun, it signicantly improves durability.
  • Component Availability: Due to the unavailability of certain components, measurements in CAD models and laser cuttings had to be approximated,causing delays in the project timeline.

  • The enclosure’s design and ideation are nearly complete. Availability of machines for bending and cutting at the IIT Delhi Workshop has been conrmed.

  • The RD Works model and CAD model for the aluminium base have been created.

  1. Introduction

The FG-1000 Function Generator is a versatile signal source designed for students, hobbyists, and developers. It provides sine, square, and triangle waveforms up to 1 MHz, with an

adjustable output voltage range from 1.5V to 10V and an offset of ±1V. This generator is powered by the Espressif Systems ESP32 microcontroller, ensuring reliability and cost- effectiveness.

  1. Technical Specifications
  • Frequency Bandwidth: Up to 1 MHz
  • Waveforms: Sine, Square, Triangle
  • Output Voltage: 1.5V to 10V (peak-to-peak)
  • DC Offset: ±1V
  • Display: 16x2 LCD for frequency and mode indication
  • Power Supply: 220V AC / 12V DC input
  • Microcontroller: ESP32-based signal processing
  1. Front Panel Diagram

Front Panel Controls:

  1. Power Button - Turns the device on/off.
  2. Waveform Selector - Chooses between sine, square, and triangle waveforms.
  3. Frequency Adjustment Buttons - Fine-tunes frequency output.
  4. Amplitude Control Knob - Adjusts signal strength.
  5. Offset Control - Adds DC offset to the waveform.
  6. LCD Display - Shows current frequency and waveform type.
  7. Output BNC Connector - Connects to external circuits and measuring instruments.
  1. Rear Panel

Rear Panel Features:

  1. Power Input (DC 12V / AC 220V) - External power supply connection.
  2. Trigger Output - Provides a TTL-compatible trigger signal.
  3. Ground Terminal - For electrical grounding.
  1. Operating Instructions Initial Setup
  1. Connect the power supply to the rear panel.
  2. Switch on the power button; the LCD display should light up.
  3. Select the desired waveform using the selector switch.

Adjusting Frequency & Amplitude

  1. Use the Frequency Adjustment Buttons to set the desired frequency.
  2. Adjust the Amplitude Control Knob to modify output voltage.
  3. Use the Offset Control to apply a DC offset to the waveform.
  1. Maintenance
  • Keep the device in a dry, dust-free environment.
  • Clean the front panel with a soft, damp cloth.
  • Do not open the casing; contact authorized service centers for repairs.
  1. Warranty & Service

This function generator comes with a one-year warranty covering manufacturing defects. For service or repairs, contact the authorized service centers listed in the report.
